## Complete Welcome Modal Copy

### Primary Scripture & Message

**"But as for me and my house, we will serve the Lord."**  
— Joshua 24:15

This ancient declaration becomes the foundation of everything that follows. Before we speak of faith, family, or finance, we must answer the fundamental question: Whom will we serve?

### Three Pillars - Reframed Through Scripture

**Faith: Know What You Believe**  
*"Build your foundation"*

In a world that worships at a thousand altars, you must know which altar is yours. Daily lessons that sharpen your convictions and deepen your roots—because a house divided against itself cannot stand, and a soul uncertain of its foundation will be swept away by every wind of doctrine.

**Family: Strengthen Your People**  
*"Intentional connection"*

Your household—whether blood or chosen—is your first congregation, your primary mission field, your sacred responsibility. Never let the important relationships drift on autopilot. To serve the Lord means to serve those He has placed in your care.

**Finance: Master Your Money**  
*"Clarity over complexity"*

Money is a servant, not a master—a tool for service, not an object of worship. Market insights that cut through the noise, because in a world that confuses having with being, you must understand both the temporal mechanics of provision and their eternal purpose.

### Supporting Scripture References (for deeper meditation)

- **Colossians 3:23** - "Whatever you do, work at it with all your heart, as working for the Lord, not for human masters."
- **Deuteronomy 6:6-7** - "These commandments that I give you today are to be on your hearts. Impress them on your children..."
- **Proverbs 27:23** - "Be sure you know the condition of your flocks, give careful attention to your herds..."
- **Matthew 6:24** - "No one can serve two masters..."

### Closing Message

This is not merely an app. This is a commitment. A declaration. A choice about whom you will serve in the ordinary moments that make up a life. Faith, family, and finance—not as three separate domains, but as three expressions of a single devotion.

**Choose this day whom you will serve.**
